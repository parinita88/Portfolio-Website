Overview

This project focused on analysing the transactions of a retail store for the time between October 2019 to October 2020.

Dataset

RFWD project provided the retail dataset in csv format. It was a simple and clean dataset, with more than 75,000 rows. The dataset had the following information on a day-to-day basis, from October 2019 to October 2020:

  • transaction_date
  • transaction_hour
  • location_state
  • location_city
  • rewards_number
  • rewards_member
  • num_of_items
  • coupon_flag
  • discount_amt
  • order_amt

Analysis

  • First, I identified important Key Performance Indicators in the data (Sales, No of Items sold, No of transactions) and I created BAN(Big Accessible Numbers) in Tableau to show summary statistics for the KPIs
  • Also, I created area charts to show the KPIs within the month at a day level, this help the executives to identify if thereâ€™s any variation in the values of KPI within a month (to see if the data points to patterns in a month like people may shopping more during any holidays in the month)
  • I also found that rewards members are accountable for a certain percentage of KPIs, which I demonstrated with pie chart.
  • Since the data had geographical locations (State and City), I decided to create maps and used map layers to identify the states and cities with most and least sales, transactions and items sold.
  • The business stakeholders might be interested in seeing the most popular and least popular cities, which I identified with help of set calculations in Tableau.
  • I also created a heatmap to identify which days and time of the day were more popular with the customers.

Dashboard

To put everything together in a single sheet, I used the dashboard feature. Here, I added interactivity. I created filters for year and month using parameters. I also allowed users to switch the metric between sales, items and transactions for the map and heatmap.

Reflection

Overall, I found working on this project to be a highly enjoyable experience. Although I get stuck sometimes with finding patterns in the data, but asking relevant questions always helps. I have realised that as I create more projects, I am becoming proficient in identifying KPIs as well as creating visuals in Tableau. I completed this project in 3 days, working around 5 hours a day. If I had more time, I would like to make the interactivity more polished with adding a menu, download options for the dashboard, etc.
